## 📌 Project Overview

Unemployment is measured by the **unemployment rate**, which represents the percentage of people who are unemployed out of the total labor force. During the **COVID-19 pandemic**, the unemployment rate increased significantly in many regions of India.

This project analyzes the **Unemployment in India dataset** to understand patterns, trends, and the impact of COVID-19 on employment using **data analysis and visualization techniques in Python**.

---

## 📂 Dataset

The dataset used in this project was downloaded from **Kaggle: Unemployment in India Dataset**.

### Dataset Features

| Feature                                 | Description                                            |
| --------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| Region                                  | State/Region of India                                  |
| Date                                    | Month and year of observation                          |
| Frequency                               | Monthly/Quarterly data                                 |
| Estimated Unemployment Rate (%)         | Percentage of unemployed people                        |
| Estimated Employed                      | Number of employed people                              |
| Estimated Labour Participation Rate (%) | Percentage of population participating in labour force |
| Area                                    | Rural or Urban                                         |

---

## 🎯 Project Objectives

* Analyze unemployment trends in India.
* Study the **impact of COVID-19 on unemployment rates**.
* Compare unemployment rates across different regions.
* Understand labour participation patterns.

---

## 🔍 Data Analysis and Visualization

The dataset was analyzed using **Pandas, Matplotlib, and Seaborn**.

Visualizations created in the project include:

* 📊 Average unemployment rate by region
* 📈 Unemployment trend over time
* 📉 Distribution of unemployment rates
* 📦 Boxplot of unemployment by area
* 📊 Labour participation rate analysis
* 📉 Employed population analysis
* 🔥 Correlation heatmap
* 📊 Rural vs Urban unemployment comparison
* 📈 Time-series unemployment visualization

These visualizations help identify patterns such as:

* Regions with **highest unemployment rates**
* Changes in unemployment during **COVID-19 lockdown periods**
* Differences between **rural and urban employment**

## 📊 Example Analysis

Some important insights obtained from the dataset:

* Unemployment increased sharply during **2020 lockdown periods**.
* Certain regions showed **consistently higher unemployment rates**.
* Rural and urban areas showed **different employment patterns**.
* Labour participation rates dropped during the pandemic.
